1. Global Automotive Market Trends

Sales Growth: Global passenger vehicle sales are projected to reach 94.7 million units in 2024, marking a 3.3% increase from 2023. This growth rate is a deceleration compared to the 11.7% observed in 2023, indicating a potential market stabilization.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Adoption: The EV market is experiencing a mild slowdown, though its sales growth remains stronger than that of internal combustion engine vehicles. In the U.S., EV sales in 2024 are expected to grow year-over-year by 16%, a decrease from approximately 64% in 2023. In China, year-over-year growth in 2024 is projected at 11.1%.

2. Competitive Landscape

Japanese Automaker Consolidation: Honda and Nissan are in discussions about a potential merger to address competitive pressures from Tesla and Chinese automakers. Such a merger would position them as the world's third-largest auto group by vehicle sales, potentially intensifying competition for Volkswagen.

Volkswagen's Performance: Volkswagen anticipates a 3% rise in car sales for 2024, a significant decline from the growth experienced in 2023. This outlook reflects a challenging economic environment and increasing competition.

3. Market Challenges and Opportunities

Pricing Dynamics: New car prices are stabilizing as supply increases, with average prices dropping from $50,300 in mid-2023 to around $49,000. Dealerships are offering greater incentives, such as rebates and low-cost financing, which now constitute 8% of the average transaction, up from 2% in 2022.

Consumer Preferences: There is a growing consumer shift towards compact cars and electric vehicles, driven by affordability concerns and environmental considerations. This trend presents opportunities for manufacturers to diversify their product lines to meet evolving consumer demands.
